Spotted Pup Pours
The Town's Finest Tea

Beloved Feedo mascot greets every guest at the door — apron on, tray in paw, tail never still

In a quiet corner of Tainan that smells faintly of roasted tea and old timber, a most unusual host welcomes every guest through the door. The museum's spotted dog — apron crisp, tray steady — has become the friendly face the whole neighborhood adores.

Witnesses report lines forming before opening. “I came for the tea and stayed for the welcome,” said one regular. The mascot simply wags in reply, offering a freshly poured cup.

“Every tea leaf remembers the heat.”

The method, it seems, is a closely guarded secret: fresh milk, roasted oolong, and a generous swirl of cream whipped into a snowy cap that stands proud above each glass.

Health inspectors who visited the premises left smiling. “Spotless,” one noted, “in every sense of the word.” The kettle, they confirmed, is always warm.
